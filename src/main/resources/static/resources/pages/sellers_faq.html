SELLERS FAQ

What is the approval process for listings? Is the listing based on subscription?

Once you submit your listing, it must be approved by BizRealWorth.com staff before it is published. This ensures that all listings are appropriate and that they follow BizRealWorth.com’s guidelines and policies. This process can take up to two business days as BizRealWorth.com conducts a corporate filing check for all businesses listed on the website. Once the listing is approved, it will be published on the website and displayed to thousands of prospective buyers worldwide.

Only once your business is approved will your subscription begin.

What if my listing is not approved?

Your listing may not be approved for two reasons:

1. Your listing does not follow our guidelines

2. Your payment could not be processed

You will be notified of the reason when your listing is not approved.

If your listing is not approved, BizRealWorth.com staff will provide feedback to help ensure that you can have your listing posted as soon as possible. Your listing will not be deleted, so you won’t have to start from scratch. And, as always, BizRealWorth.com staff are on hand to provide any assistance you may need throughout the process. Read through BizRealWorth.com’s business listings guidelines for more information.

What are the guidelines for listings?

BizRealWorth.com's business listing guidelines are as follows:

1. Business must be incorporated or registered as an LLC (BizRealWorth.com is unable facilitate the sale of sole proprietorship businesses).

2. DBA businesses are required to provide their primary company name followed by the DBA (both names remain private).

3. Listing title and content must not contain the business’ or seller’s name.

4. No inappropriate or unprofessional content.

5. Listing must not contain off-platform contact information (e.g., email, telephone number, address).

6. No excessive use of attention-grabbing language or punctuation (i.e., ALL CAPS, ***, !!!!!, etc.).

All listings are approved at BizRealWorth.com's discretion.

Use of graphics and images for listings?

The most response a listing receives is the one which has good high quality images of the business. Images enhances the listing as it appeals to a buyer because they can then visualize a future with your company. The following are the do’s and don’t to consider before uploading an image to your listing:

Do:

- Include photos of your storefront (business name will be blurred if visible)

- Include photos of your product(s), if applicable

- Include photos of company events, etc.

Don’t:

- Select blurry images

- Select photos that look like marketing ads

- Select photos that reveal sensitive information about your business (the business’s name, street address, contact information, etc.)

If you do not wish to submit a photo, BizRealWorth.com will upload a public domain image for the listing.

Which factors to consider while writing a listing?

-Include accurate financial information

-Always include a LISTING PRICE, if possible. (Check out our Business Valuation Products to help you determine the correct listing price)

-Always Include high definition images of your business.

-A well written Title for your posting with detailed business description

Firstly, advertise your listing price if possible. Check out our business valuation calculator to get started. Secondly, there are four categories in total that appear with each listing: Gross Revenue, EBITDA(Profit), Cash Flow, and FF&E(Furniture, Fixtures, & Equipment).

While it is not mandatory to provide information for each of these fields, doing so indicates to buyers that you have a good understanding of your company’s finances. Below are definitions of each category:

1. Gross Revenue: Total income the business received before any expenses have been subtracted.

2. Profit: The amount of earnings/income realized after expenses are subtracted from total revenue.

3. Cash Flow: Net amount of cash moving in and out of the business. A positive cash flow suggests the company's liquid assets are increasing, which enables it to pay debts, provide a return to shareholders, and provide a security net against future financial challenges, among other things. The opposite is true for negative cash flow.

4. FF&E: Value of Furniture, Fixtures, and Equipment that will remain with the business. Ex: desks, computers, machinery, etc. This will vary given the type of business.
